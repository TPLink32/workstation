Lua Books

The following are books containing information solely, or in part, on Lua. Ideally these are published and have an ISBN. Please don't list works in progress.

Beginning Lua Programming

  • Beginning Lua Programming by Kurt Jung and AaronBrown. Paperback: 672 pages, Publisher: Wrox (February 12, 2007), Language: English, ISBN-10: 0470069171, ISBN-13: 978-0470069172.
  • Written to be usable by beginners, but we think more experienced programmers will also find it useful. It covers:
    • the entire Lua 5.1 language (including the built-in libraries; differences with Lua 5.0 are also noted)
    • interfacing Lua with C and other languages
    • a variety of community-written libraries
    • introductions to using Lua with and for: databases, web and network programming, game programming, and handheld devices
  • More info:
    • [Amazon] -- with "Search Inside!" feature; [Barnes & Noble]; publisher sites: [Wiley], [Wrox] -- including samples: Chapter 1, the table of contents, and the index.
    • [a thread] and [another thread] on lua-l
